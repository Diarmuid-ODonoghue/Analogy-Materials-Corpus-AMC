Once upon a time, there was a very possessive cat who was best friends with a rabbit. The cat would often say to the rabbit, "You can't play with anyone else but me." One day, the cat went on a trip and the rabbit had no one to play with. But then a squirrel came to visit the rabbit. The squirrel brought a ball along and said, "Would you like to play catch with me?" The rabbit was excited and said "Yes." The squirrel and the rabbit had a great time playing catch together. When the cat returned and found the rabbit playing with someone else, he got very angry. He hissed, "I'll never play with you again!" The cat was so angry that he jumped onto the squirrel's back. But the squirrel began to run, and the cat found himself sliding off the squirrel's back. The cat was very scared. The squirrel saw the cat's distress and stopped running. The cat realized that being possessive only got him into trouble. It is better to have two friends instead of
